The problem

The kitchen is a daily decision engine — without one.

Inventory, recipes, lists, and budgets are disconnected.
People buy food they already have, forget what's about to expire, and cook around what's loud — not what's optimal.
Households overbuy and waste — quietly, expensively.
Industry research suggests roughly a third of food bought at home is thrown away. Most of it is invisible until the bin.
Existing apps solve isolated pieces.
Recipe apps don't know your fridge. Grocery apps don't know your pantry. Budget apps don't know what you actually used. Nothing closes the loop.
The solution

Friddy: a Kitchen OS that closes the loop.

Inventory, expiry, recipes, grocery planning, budgeting, and price optimization — connected as a single decision engine.

How it works

Six steps. One decision loop.

01
Add or scan groceries
Snap a receipt, scan a barcode, or photograph the haul. Friddy catalogs what came in.
02
Track inventory & expiry
Quantities, locations, and freshness windows update silently in the background.
03
Recommend meals
Weighted by what's about to expire, what you like, and your dietary rules.
04
Build optimized lists
What's missing, what's cheaper where, all inside your weekly budget.
05
You shop smarter
One list, one route, no duplicate spending. Receipt scan closes the loop.
06
Friddy learns
Each week the model gets sharper on your purchases, waste, and taste.
Market

High-frequency. High-pain. Underserved by software.

Food, grocery, and household budgeting are daily problems for every household on earth. Friddy starts from one painful daily moment — "what should I do with what I have?" — and earns a path to the broader essential-decision platform from there.
